How a 13-Year-Old Wrote a Fantasy Novel

By Praanav J Thaakur (parent/guardian-managed) · 2026-06-26

The Preditorium Prophecy was written by Praanav J Thaakur, a 13-year-old debut author. It began with a world (the Preditorium), grew into six friends with strange powers, and became a real, finished 183-page novel — proof that a big idea plus the discipline to finish can become a published book.

People are often surprised when they learn the author is in school. So here is the honest story of how the book came together — and what any young writer can take from it.

Who is Praanav J Thaakur?

Praanav is a 13-year-old reader who loves fantasy worlds, mysteries, monsters and big "what if" ideas. The Preditorium Prophecy: The Golden Statue is his debut novel.

It started with a world, not a plot

The first thing that existed was the place: the Preditorium, a hidden world with tunnels, guardians and secrets. Then came the characters — six friends called the Ascendants, each with an unusual power (and one very unlucky monster magnet named Cody). The prophecy gave them a reason to move.

The hard part: finishing

Anyone can start a story. The real lesson behind this book is finishing one. That meant writing in small, regular sessions, pushing through the messy middle, and accepting that a first draft is allowed to be imperfect. Twenty-one chapters and 183 pages later, it was a real book.

What young writers can borrow from this

  • Build a world you would want to live in — characters and plot grow from it.
  • Write a little, often. Momentum beats marathon bursts.
  • Let the first draft be rough. You cannot edit a blank page.
  • Finish. A finished imperfect book beats a perfect unfinished one.

If you know a young person with a big idea, that is exactly who this book was written for — and proof that the idea can become real.
